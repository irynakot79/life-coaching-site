When duty replaces desire

About the quiet way we lose ourselves by trying to be good.

Sometimes you are praised for being reliable at the very moment you are quietly disappearing. Duty can look like strength from the outside, and, from the inside, feel like a soft distance — from yourself, from what you once wanted.

This isn't against responsibility. It is a slower question — one that can only be asked without hurry. Which choices here come from a fear of disappointing someone? And which still carry a living yes?

Noticing the difference doesn't set anything right at once. It simply gives your own voice a little of its weight back.

These are general notes, not a substitute for a caring conversation. If something lands heavily, please share it with someone close by.
